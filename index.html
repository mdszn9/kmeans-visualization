How does K-means work?

1. Initialize: place K centroids (here using k-means++ by default).
2. Assign: each point joins the cluster of its nearest centroid (Euclidean distance).
3. Update: each centroid moves to the mean of its assigned points.
Repeat 2 & 3 until no point changes cluster (convergence).

Inertia is the sum of squared distances from points to their centroid. K-means minimizes it — but only finds a local minimum, so initialization matters.
